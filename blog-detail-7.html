How To Create An Online Course From Scratch.

By lorem ipsum
Nov 10,2021

There has been a steady increase in the number of people looking to sign up for online learning programs for the past couple of years. The reasons for the aforementioned fact can range from an inability to invest in a traditional classroom program to a desire to gain new skills from the comfort of one's home. In the wake of the novel coronavirus pandemic in the year 2020, there was a large surge in the number of people applying for online courses. Needless to say, the eLearning industry witnessed a huge increase in market share. According to a research report, the global E-learning market was estimated at USD 144 Billion in 2019 and is expected to reach USD 374.3 Billion by 2026. The global E-learning Market is expected to grow at a compound annual growth rate (CAGR) of 14.6% from 2019 to 2026 [1]. So if you are someone who is considering joining the flourishing online education industry, this is a fantastic time to jump in. Before beginning a new venture, it is natural for a person to have several doubts and concerns. This holds for educators looking to utilise technology to further share their expertise with interested students. Some of the questions that might arise in the mind of someone looking to create an online course are:

  1. How to start creating an online course?
  2. What topic should I build my online course on?
  3. Which online learning platforms can publish my course?
  4. What online courses are in demand?
  5. How long is a good online course supposed to be?

This guide will help you make the initial steps in your journey of creating your online course and hopefully aid you in creating a widely successful online program. So let's dive right into the major steps you need to undertake-

1. Select your course topic:

The first step of designing your online course is to choose the course topic. Take the following points into important consideration while doing so-

a) Play to your strengths:

Begin by asking yourself the following questions:

  • What is an area in which your friends, family or colleagues repeatedly ask for your expertise?
  • Is this an area of study where you are at a considerable advantage over others?
  • If you answered yes to the previous question, now ask yourself if you are passionate about that area of study.

It is imperative that you feel strongly motivated about the subject you decide. The process of creating an online program can feel long and daunting, especially if you are a novice. If you feel passionate about teaching others about your selected subject it will keep you motivated and push you forward into completing it. If you love what you teach it will drive you into thinking of out-of-the-box ways of creating a learning journey that is totally unique and a worthwhile venture for people to invest in. Besides, if you are not passionate about the course topic that does come across the students and the engagement value of your course takes a hit.

b)Determining your topic's demand in the market:

Another important aspect before selecting your course topic is to establish if the subject you are offering is one which people are interested in learning. If you are looking to earn some capital through your online course you should also consider the profit potential of the subject. Actively research whether people are willing to invest their time and/or money into the subject you want to teach. The average sales price for courses in our dataset was $182.59 across various e-learning platforms. However, the median course price was found to be $76.50 [4]. Selecting your online course topic is the foundational building block of your program. Hence it is essential that you think very carefully about these initial steps.

2. Research and Structure the Course:

The next aspect of your online course creation process is the research and planning phase. It is important to research the subject extensively even if you have decades of experience in the subject. Knowledge constantly evolves and you need to evolve along with it. The key point here is to note down all the sub-topics you are interested in including in the course and also writing down the learning goals for your course modules. You can follow, for example, this course structure:

Course subject: Building fundamentals of English Grammar

-Main topic 1: Parts of Speech & Grammar Terminology

  • Subtopic: Sentence Structure and Formation
  • Subtopic: Introduction to Parts of Speech
  • Sub-Subtopic: Nouns, Pronouns, Verbs, Adjectives, Adverbs,Prepositions, Interjections and Conjunctions

– Main topic 2: Rules and Usage

  • Subtopic: Active and Passive Voice
  • Subtopic: Practices to improve grammar

The organisation of the subject matter is of utmost importance as an organised lesson plan is an effective method in creating a positive impact on the students. You can also invest your time in performing keyword research to figure out what words related to your subject people frequently search for. You can then choose to include some of these sought after queries. As an additional step, you can also look into what your would-be competitors are offering and decide on how you can set your service apart from theirs. You can do this by looking at their course layout and brainstorming about the additional features that you could incorporate in your course. Ask yourself what additional content you would want from this course topic if you were a student,take note of those points and add them to your course outline.

Spend an ample amount of time in figure structure the course. Decide on the course layout, the main topics and the sub-topics and also plan how you mean to incorporate some engaging activities in the course. Some of the examples of engaging activities could be:

  • An assignment relating to different sub-topics
  • A task for the students to create a project based on the lesson.
  • You can design mini-quizzes so that students can check their comprehension of the subject along the way.
  • Making student pairs and assigning each pair a different case-study

Design your lesson layout carefully because it is most likely that you would be displaying it to your target audience. Hence it is essential that your potential students find it well-ordered and easy to navigate. If you need some help in designing a template for your course you can read LearnWorld's blog post titled "18 Amazing Outline Templates to use in Course Design" [3].

3. Creating the Course Material

After you are done with planning and structuring your program you can begin focussing on creating and developing the online course content. You can incorporate the study material in form of simple text, animated slides, or even engaging video lessons. There are several ways to get your material across and it really just depends on your creative skills and what you think the best medium is. Ultimately the method of delivery you go with will decide the kind of media production services you would need.

But how do you get started with creating the actual content?

If you are a subject matter expert and have been in the teaching industry for a while, then you probably have some of your old content with you. This content might be in the forms of lecture notes, blog posts, presentations, assignments or tests. Maybe you also have access to some screen recordings of your old lectures or a video demo of some practical experiment you might have performed. Whatever old material you have access to can be repurposed and used in your new course. This will save you a huge amount of time in the content creation department and you can invest the time saved during the production cycle.

However, if you are starting from scratch and don't have any old material to refer to or maybe you simply can't locate the old content that is perfectly fine too! In this case, all you need to do to get started is make a skills checklist. Follow the steps given to make one:

  1. Write down a list of skills that you hope to deliver to your students through your online program
  2. Go through the list and group the skills based on similarity in skill type
  3. Now divide the skill types based on the level of complexity.

With the basic framework of the content set, you can now fill in the study material using old resources, new material from different books, the internet etc.

The Production Stage:

Fortunately, with today's technology, you have access to many tools that facilitate an easy production cycle for your online program. Plan your time expeditiously and as per your requirements and you will have a functional online program in no time. It is usually suggested that you spend between 2 weeks to 2 months in the production stage. You can then invest a week or two into the editing process of the post-production stage. You can refer to VdoCipher's "101 Tools for Online Course Creators to Create, Publish & Monetize Courses" [2] to gain an idea about the various assistive tools that are available for online course creators. You are finally done with all the hard work of developing a course. Now it is time to decide on how you want to bring the course to the masses. If you have a website for your business then you can choose to upload your program there. The options of launching the course on your own website or selling it on some online learning platform have their own sets of pros and cons. Hosting your online program on your professional website would offer you greater autonomy and more flexibility. You would be in charge of handling the subscription policy and pricing of the courses you offer. However, the responsibility of managing the marketing campaign, accommodating different payment methods, converting your target audience into actual customers also falls on your shoulders. On the other hand, if you choose to publish your course on an online education platform like EZColab, you would have an easier time in setting up your course, you would have access to several top-notch technical features that EZColab offers. Opting for other e-learning platforms like Udemy, SkillShare, Coursera etc will also allow you to invest more time on course creation and less in brainstorming about the advertising and marketing aspects of the job.On the other hand, there are significant disadvantages to using established platforms for publishing your courses as well. You would have restrictive pricing options and you would not be provided with access to audience metrics. One of the biggest disadvantages would be that your course would be placed next to similar courses, resulting in your program being overshadowed, resulting in a loss of profit for you.

It is advised that you conduct thorough research of these pros and cons and go with the alternative that serves a greater benefit to your professional life goals. 5. Cultivate an Online Learning Community:

A fantastic way of offering your students an amazing online learning experience is to create a community for your online course. It has long been established that while online education has many advantages, it also brings a set of major drawbacks. drawbacks One of these drawbacks is the prevailing sense of isolation between students and their teachers. An excellent way to combat this problem is to encourage communication among students who are enrolled in the same learning program.

An online community of learners can promote peer-to-peer interaction that can lead to the promotion of healthy competition among students and also ignite a passion for continuous learning. The presence of such a community can also prove to be a respite for the instructor as they would not have to shoulder the responsibility to answer every query that a student might have. Instead, the students can initiate interaction among themselves by discussing their doubts and questions with each other, offering their viewpoints and opinions and having harmonious discussions. A constructive, vibrant conversation between students can greatly increase the excitement in your online course. Another advantage is that students can use this platform to offer genuine feedback which is very important for any instructor. Therefore fostering an online community of students will positively impact the overall experience of your online course and consequently result in a larger magnitude of success.

There are various ways you can establish a student community for your course:

  1. Create chat rooms on your website
  2. Create discussion forums and invite the students to join them
  3. Make group chats on the social media platform of your choice

It can be a difficult process to carry out these processes by yourself, hence using the services of an online communication platform like EZColab, for building your student community, is an excellent idea.

6. Get Feedback, Improve & Update:

Constructive feedback is an integral part of any teaching endeavour. The purpose of teaching is to educate students and familiarise them with new knowledge that they previously were strangers to. However, if a teacher keeps on giving out lectures and study materials without taking a break to determine whether the students were comprehending any of the topics, it would be a sheer waste of time and energy for both the parties involved.

Having an effective feedback mechanism associated with your online course can help you check in with your students. It can help you understand how much of the subject the students are retaining and if they are having difficulty in coping with the pace of the lesson plan or any of your teaching methods. Do not be disheartened if you get some complaints about your course. No learning program is a "one size fits all" solution and different students will have different learning capacities. You might find that you will have to think of creative ways to adapt to the needs of different students. You could simply speed up or slow down the pace of the course to adjust to the needs of the students. Another way of adapting to the feedback could be to create student pairs wherein you pair a high-performing student with a student who might be struggling in understanding the course.

Another important demographic that you can rely on for feedback is that of your fellow educators. You can call upon your colleagues or collaborate with other similar subject matter experts to weigh in on your online course. Being teachers themselves, they would have an eye for recognising some mistakes you might have made or some suggestions on how to improve the quality of the content.

It is imperative that you take all the feedback you receive into consideration, think of ways to improve your course according to it, implement those changes, then ask for feedback again and repeat!

Conclusion:

You are now at the end of this comprehensive guide and hopefully, you have a clearer idea of how to get started on designing a brand new online course! Now all you are required to do is take the first step and get started. Creating your own online course may sound like a daunting task now but if you follow this step-by-step guide carefully you will find it to be an enjoyable experience. Here's to hoping you build an incredibly successful course in the near future!
