The Biggest Drawbacks of Online Learning

By lorem ipsum
Nov 10,2021

Ever since the COVID-19 pandemic started raging havoc on the entire world the number of people resorting to online learning platforms has greatly increased. Thousands of students and working professionals took to popular and reliable educational platforms like Coursera, Udemy, Skillshare, LinkedIn Learning among others, that provide an online environment in order to make productive use of their time and to expand their knowledge while they had the opportunity. If you refuse to accept this fact at face value, take a moment to look at the bevy of impressive statistics behind it. As of 2020, the market for mobile e-learning had grown by $38 billion dollars. According to a study by KPMG in India, the Indian online education market is set to grow to USD 1.96 billion and around 9.6 million users by 2021 from USD 247 million and around 1.6 million users in 2016. Reskilling and online certifications are the largest category in 2021 and stand at USD 93 million. Primary and secondary supplemental education will be the largest category by the end of 2021 at USD 773 million, growing at a compound annual growth rate of 60%. Competitive test preparation will be the fastest-growing category in 2021, growing at a compound annual growth rate of an astounding 64%. Therefore, it is safe to say that the growth rate for online learning will not be relapsing any time soon.

With the ever-growing accessibility of the internet and the rising popularity of online learning platforms, there has been a fierce debate about the benefits and drawbacks of e-learning as opposed to those of offline learning. The conventional method of teaching, which is referred to as offline learning, does not require an internet connection and therefore has a major advantage over online education. This provides a benefit to those people who do not have internet access. It also provides a sense of familiarity to interested learners. It involves having a teacher present in the classroom to moderate the students during the lesson and to help maintain discipline and focus. However, there has been a growing trend towards online education platforms due to the ease of access to resources and flexibility they provide especially in a time where physically attending a class is fraught with peril. A host of educational institutes including schools. tuition centers, coaching centers, and colleges have adopted the online environment to ensure an uninterrupted journey to an interested learner.

This is not to say that digital learning does not come with its own set of drawbacks. Before getting on board with distance learning and enrolling yourself in an online course it is imperative that you are aware of the problems that might not always be talked about but still plague this industry. Only then is it advised that you decide between opting for a traditional education or an online program. Here is a carefully compiled list of the drawbacks of online education:

1. Lack of Communication between Student and Teacher -

Though Education Technology companies are constantly researching new ways to improve their software programs there is no denying the communication gap that develops between the instructor and the students. Often lectures don't feel like an open discussion and present no avenue for the students to voice any doubt or opinion that they might have regarding the subject. Online courses are also optimally scheduled such that an instructor can cover a maximum number of classes in an efficient way, this of course means that time is of the essence, and as soon as one lecture ends the instructor might have to join another session just as quickly. You will come across many people in your life who confidently maintain that self-study is the only step required to completely and efficiently master the education you are receiving, but there is no doubt that having a functional and healthy relationship with your teacher will have a positive impact on you. In this aspect, traditional learning environments have an obvious advantage over the online learning environment.

2. Lack of Feedback-

Traditional learning provides the subject matter experts with the opportunity to give feedback to the students directly in the class. Such personalized feedback can offer great insights to the students and help motivate them as well. Similarly, in a physical classroom, people can voice ideas that they think can help improve the learning process. The pupils are in fact encouraged to put forth their suggestions to the teacher as this helps boost the interaction level in the class. On the other hand, when it comes to online lectures oftentimes there are no means for the students to provide feedback on the quality of the online class. This disadvantage becomes even more problematic when a student faces genuine obstacles in understanding the course curriculum but has no way of approaching their instructor. As a result, these queries might keep on piling up significantly affecting the learning of the student.

3. Sense of Isolation-

Another drawback associated with learning an online program is the feeling of isolation that students might experience. The methods of teaching currently being used in e-learning platforms do not facilitate a lot of human-to-human interaction which can lead to the feeling of social isolation especially at the time of a global pandemic. There is often no provision of a digital space where the online students can virtually assemble to have a friendly and productive interaction with each other. These digital spaces can also be utilized to initiate class discussions wherein students can exchange ideas and viewpoints pertaining to the subject being studied. In addition to this lack of peer-to-peer interaction the attribute of remoteness, which is another characteristic of the online environment, can also result in the students being easily demotivated since there would be a lack of friendly competition between them. To combat this problem, a few institutions have initiated blended learning techniques that encourage face-to-face interaction among students with the help of video conferencing and also scheduling regular discussion forums to maintain personal contact.

4. Requirement of Self-Motivation Skills-

To overcome all the drawbacks mentioned above it is crucial that an individual believes he or she is equipped with the tenacity to see things all the way through before signing up for virtual learning. When we say that you need serious self-motivation skills we mean that it is absolutely imperative that you have them. In a traditional classroom setting a student has an easier time maintaining his or her focus on the course material. Advantages like face-to-face conversations with the instructors, the option to raise doubts and opinions in class, and interaction with fellow students all contribute to making the offline learning environment an engaging one. These also help a student in adhering to the strict deadlines provided by most online schools by developing excellent time management skills. However, since an online learning environment fails to facilitate these things the online student is left to his own devices. Only with consistent, never-ending determination and unyielding self-motivation can online students keep up with the challenging tasks of online courses.

5. Online Programs are Limited to Certain Subjects-

Between the alternatives of doing a course on natural history or one on how to perform a medical autopsy, which one do you reckon will be more beneficial to an individual student? Hopefully, we have the same answer here which is the former option. All disciplines in education are not at par with each other and therefore not all online programs can cater to their students in a fully efficient manner. Some fields of study naturally require a more hands-on approach for example medicine, engineering sciences, aviation, etc. while others like humanities and social sciences seem to be a better fit for the online mode of education. Because after all, you would not want your doctor to perform an operation on you when he only ever had performed an operation in a simulated environment or hire a product engineer who has had no exposure to how real-world industries operate. Complicated subjects and practices that demand a physical environment cannot be fully transformed into the online method of teaching. Online degrees offered in theoretical subjects that have more repetitive topics and little to no requirement of practice-based learning prove to be excellent choices for online courses. Of course, this argument is subject to change in the future depending on the level of technological innovations that humanity reaches to. There might well come a time when all disciplines in education can be efficiently taught by online lessons.
