An essential guide to formulating the perfect content creation strategy

By lorem ipsum
Nov 10,2021

The rise of social media, the birth of blogger/influencer culture and the fact that these days people are consuming more digital content than ever, has led to myriads of sources of content on the internet. This has put increased pressure on content creators to come up with the best, fool-proof practices for creating content that is not only engaging but also impactful. It is rightly proclaimed that "Content is King" and therefore it should be the objective of every content creator that their digital material adds value to their customer's life. This can seem like a daunting task if you're just venturing into this realm. We have compiled a comprehensive list of the best practices for online content creation to provide you with a little boost in your endeavours.

1. Know your Target Audience

"Target" is the key term here. Contrary to what you might believe writing for a broad range of the audience is not an effective content strategy. It can actually result in the opposite direction of attaining the primary goal, which is to gain traffic and other objectives like increasing sales, boosting the number of subscribers, lead generation etc. Before you start creating content, do thorough research about the kind of audience profile you want to attract. Once you have locked in this information you can start customising your content to cater to their needs and expectations. Many individuals and organisations like to utilise Key Performance Indicators (KPIs) to perform this task. The dictionary definition of a KPI is that it is a measurable goal that demonstrates how effectively the business objectives of an organisation are being realised. Content creators can use various metrics to determine the type of audience they are attracting the most, the list of their most visited and least visited pages, behaviour patterns etc. Some of the most valuable KPIs are:

  • Sales Revenue
  • Online Marketing ROI
  • Site Traffic to Lead Ratio
  • Number of shares
  • Client Retention Rate

To gain a more comprehensive understanding of KPIs refer to Salesforce Pardot's article titled "14 Content Marketing KPIs You Should Be Tracking" [1].

If you ever encounter a roadblock while writing content always remember that you are producing this content solely to satisfy your potential customers. Put yourself in their shoes for a moment and think of how you would want this particular product/service information to be conveyed to you. Perhaps you would create a Question and Answer segment, write a descriptive blog post or maybe produce engaging visual content to get your message across. It ultimately depends on how creatively you develop valuable content to adhere to the customers' needs and your business goals.

2. Engage in Keyword Search

The purpose of spending a huge amount of time and energy in publishing relevant content is to attract views or "eyeballs" as it is sometimes referred to in the marketing world. To attract more visitors to your business you need to invest significant time in keyword research. This is the foundational step for Search Engine Optimisation (SEO)[3]. Always keep SEO in mind as it is a heavily employed technique used in digital marketing to increase the number of prospects that find your content through a search engine.

Begin by researching the most searched keywords related to your topic, also determine what the least searched keywords are. Also, keep track of related keywords that do not seem to have a lot of competition. Most often than not short tail keywords have fierce competition while long tail keywords are often seldom used. You can use this lack of competition to your advantage and incorporate the long tail keywords in your content. For example, if you are a computer science teacher who is trying to get subscribers for their online C++ course you would probably find that keywords such as "c++ for beginners" have high monthly searches whereas "certified online course on c++ programming for beginners" might not. Using lesser-used long tail keywords is an excellent way of improving your search ranking and getting more leads, subscribers and/or customers.

There are multiple services available on the internet to help you perform this task. These include both free and paid services. Google Keyword Planner [4] is an excellent free tool for researching, discovering and analysing relevant keywords. If you have the capital to invest in paid keyword search tools you can opt for SEMrush [5] which is a highly popular tool that claims unmatched and superior performance. One noteworthy point here is to not restrict yourself to one such service. Make sure you employ the use of numerous keyword search tools because results from different services are known to vary from time to time. Search the web for ideas on how to optimise the title of your content piece, scroll through social media platforms to determine what hashtags are trending and customise your content according to your findings. Most importantly, do not use popular keywords just for the sake of using them, make sure they are relevant to your topic and fit the context well. You can refer to Brian Dean's article titled "15 BEST Keyword Research Tools for SEO " [2] to get information about the various keyword search tools that are currently available."

3. Put a Unique Touch to your Content

We have established the powerful role that SEO plays in the content creation process. There are many SEO tools available in the market that you can use to increase your SEO score. A simple way of obtaining a higher SEO score is publishing unique content. Unique content refers to content that is original and has not been plagiarised. Unique content sets you apart from other sources that are giving out similar information to the audience and hence greatly increases the chances of your content getting the spotlight.

Let us understand this better through an example. Consider a scenario wherein a potential customer searches for "certified training in web development with python" via a search engine. The search engine will then employ complex algorithms to search for the best sources that offer an answer to the user's search query and provide a list of helpful content that is going to be ranked in order of decreasing relevance and content quality. There are going to be various other organisations and independents that will be offering their services, to cut through the competition you would have to make sure that your content is unique and has not been duplicated from anywhere else. You can accomplish this by mentioning that you provide a free 3-day trial period or that you conduct live doubt sessions at the end of every week. You are essentially providing unique incentives to your potential customers for picking your service over your competitors' services

Search engines levy heavy penalties on sources that try to cheat their algorithms with duplicated data. To avoid any such penalties we suggest the following points

  1. Utilise your own writing style to come up with quality content. Do not try to copy the writing style of other content creators, this will only backfire in your effort to rank better.
  2. Do not try to imitate the content format of your competitors. Create content keeping specific channels in mind
  3. Use your own reasoning and logical abilities to explain why your product/service is superior to others.
  4. If possible, use your own original audio clips and video clips. The more fresh your pieces of content are the better they will rank in the search engine indexing process.
4. Gradually get into the workflow

It has been observed that a number of people who already have settled upon a content idea struggle with getting started with the actual content creation process. The introductory part of your content can be the most challenging to finalise. It requires a lot of brainstorming and editing, therefore it is generally recommended to not fret about it too much and start with the piece of content you are most sure of. Do not worry about dividing the material into various segments and pick individual segments to work on. Once you start writing and creating content you will gradually get into the flow of things and bring the entire work to completion.

Once you are done with the first draft make sure that you proofread, perform a content audit and look for any errors that you might have made. You can also ask for someone else's help for this as long as they have some knowledge in the subject. You will find that there are quite a few modifications to be done and this will help you in producing the final cut of your ace quality content.

5. Work on your Content Marketing Strategy

Search engines like Google, Yahoo etc. will not rank your content at the top right at the beginning, it is going to take some time for you to get there. Till the time that happens, you can focus on content promotion through social media. You can research the hashtags that your target audience is using and then come up with kinds of content that are on-brand with your product/service. You can use creative ways to carry out the promotion drive depending upon the social media platform you are using, for instance:

  1. If you plan to do an Instagram Live session then you need to make sure you host an interactive and engaging session so as to persuade people to sign up for your product/service.
  2. If you have published some articles or blogs on your product/service you can repurpose them and share them as social media posts and as part of your emails. Just make sure they are not very wordy and accompany the posts with some compelling visuals.
  3. You can also opt for content advertising if you want to have a successful content marketing campaign handled by a professional content team.
  4. Always make use of accurate titles so that search engines can pick up your content easily.

You will find that over time the bounce rate of your content will decrease and its ranking will gradually improve. Lastly, it is important that you keep updating your content with trending keywords to attract traffic and work on content amplification by publishing content that is meaningful and relevant to your subject.
