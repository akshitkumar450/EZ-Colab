How To Create An Online Course From Scratch.

By lorem ipsum
Nov 10,2021

Are you a course creator who finds themselves getting increasingly flustered while thinking about attaching the right price tag to their course? If yes, you have landed on the right page. By the end of this article, you will have a clear idea about the best approach to deciding the price of your online course.

Most course creators grapple with finding the sweet spot in online course pricing where they get to achieve their profit goals and their loyal customers are still satisfied with the product. The reason so many course instructors struggle with this step is because, like most entrepreneurs, they realize how crucial it is to select the right price for their course. This is a decision that should be taken after a lot of strategic planning and not by estimated guesswork. The crux of the significance of a pricing strategy is that the higher you can price your course without causing a substantial drop in sales volume, the more revenue you will generate. Also, if you raise prices while keeping your costs, including the marketing spend and cost of your email campaign, constant, the more money you will make.

Before we jump onto the steps you should keep in mind while deciding on a profitable online course price, let us take a look at the three different price tiers you can adopt.

1. Free Courses:

Many educational platforms like Udemy, Coursera, Skillshare etc. host numerous free courses. Some of the commonly listed reasons for educators to offer free courses are:

  1. Course creators who publish their pilot course on their websites decide to advertise it through their email list in order to build trust with their subscribers
  2. Established course creators offer free courses as a means to generate more leads
  3. Free content can be seen as a good way to increase the SEO of the creator's website consequently resulting in more website traffic
  4. In order to sell another service or product, instructors sometimes include a free course as bonus content in exchange for customers signing up for that other service or product. For instance, if the instructor has designed a 6-week summer training program, they might offer one of their courses as an incentive for people to register for their training program.
2. Paid Courses:

Paid courses can be found everywhere. In this category, when we say "paid courses" we refer to the Pay-per-course model. Interested students pay for the individual courses that they want to sign-up for. The range of prices for these courses is diverse and one can find a course at almost any and every price point. This model provides immediate revenue generation and empowers the customers as they get to pay only for the course that they want to sign-up for.

3. Subscription/Membership model

Subscriptions and Memberships are online educational models where people can subscribe to, or take membership of, a service by paying instalments on a monthly or yearly basis. Course creators offer one or all of their courses continually as long as the members are paying their due. There are a lot of membership pricing plans that can be offered to the customers as well as amazing financial benefits. This model also offers a recurring revenue stream for your business and is therefore gaining popularity with many course creators. If you want to learn more about this education model, click here to read KJ McGowan's article titled "5 Secrets to Sell Courses Online with Subscriptions" [1].

Now that we are acquainted with the three available pricing options, let us dive right into understanding how course creators, such as yourself, should go about pricing their online courses.

1. Define Your Income Goals

Having a clear strategy in mind is imperative before you start selecting the price of your online course. After all, the price point will drive many crucial factors such as:

  1. Increasing penetration in the current market
  2. Entering a new market
  3. Establishing your brand name
  4. Impacting the revenue you will earn

Course creation is an excellent way of earning a living. It is a noble endeavour, after all, you are offering a well-structured form of high-quality information to interested people so that they can benefit from it. This being said, course creation is also a time-consuming process, naturally, you would expect to earn a substantial income from it. Different course creators may have different income goals in mind. Some might be planning to turn this online business as their primary income source while others may see the online course sales as a source of passive income.

Start by determining your revenue goals. Think about how much you would like to make in a yearly income and then break that down to what you would need to make on a monthly basis. This will help you figure out the approximate price you would need to charge for your course. Next, think about all the hours you put in and all the added expenses that went into the production stage of the course creation process. This should provide you with some clarity on the optimal price of your course.

2. Determine the Value of Your Course Outcomes

Think long and hard about the value your course will deliver to your students. It is the course outcomes that will help you in determining what your carefully designed online course is worth.

If a student successfully learns and applies what you are teaching, how valuable will the outcome be for them? Will your potential students have a chance to make more money now that they have additional skills in their repertoire? Can they expand their personal or professional brand? You don't need to have a quantifiable answer to these questions, only a definite one. Like we said before, think long and hard about the positive value that will be attributed to the students and then put a price tag on them.

While you are brainstorming about these positive outcomes, take a moment to talk to your potential customers. You can do this by using your email list to reach out to people and ask them to participate in surveys or interviews pertaining to their views on the price value of course outcomes. Analyse these responses and integrate your own reasoning into them.

Finally, determine the minimum value that you are certain that your course is worth, and take into consideration the following two parameters before arriving on the actual price you would be setting for the course:

  1. A high return on investment that the prospective customers would achieve
  2. A decent rate of return for you

By now, it must be pretty clear that setting an online course price is not as straightforward a process as one might assume it to be, however, it can be effectively done. Put in sincere efforts at this stage of your course creation journey and your online course business is sure to benefit from it in the long run.

3. Perform Industry Research

A common piece of advice that we are sure you will stumble upon on the Internet is to research the price range that your competitors are offering and then assign the median value to your course. This would be a bad idea. Let us understand why.

Engaging in industry research and finding out what other professionals in your industry are charging for their courses is indeed a good idea to get an estimated price that online learners are willing to pay. However, basing the price value of your course solely according to that is not a wise move. The factors that should dictate the price tag on a course are the value outcomes that the students will benefit from and the expertise and guidance that you will be putting in to help them achieve those benefits.

Apply the same concept that you apply at the beginning of your course creation journey when you research your competitors to gain insights into what course outcomes they have put on offer or what unique selling points are making the audience sign-up for the courses. Research your competition to simply gain insights on the different price points and to validate the market demand of your topic.

It is also possible that you have a totally new online course idea and therefore have no existing competitors to perform the preliminary industry research. Do not worry, trust the pioneer in you and be the first person to set a premium price on that course topic. In fact, you can increase the perceived value of your course by leveraging the scarcity and highlighting the unique benefits of enrolling in your course and even aim towards setting a higher price point if you are sure that there is decent market demand.

4. Do Not Underprice Your Course

If you are planning to sell your first online course you probably have had the following thoughts centred around lower pricing in your mind:

  1. What if my audience is not willing to pay the ideal price for the course?
  2. My course is not as long as my competitors', I should charge less than the average price.

    3. ..or the most common of all thoughts:

  3. If I charge less than my competitors, that will give me an advantage over my competition and increase my returns.

Let's discuss these common conundrums one at a time.

  1. "What if my audience is not willing to pay the ideal price for the course?"

    2. Remember that a good online course has value. And people are always willing to pay for something that is valuable. If you are sure that you have created a course that will intrigue your audience by teaching them something that is new, fresh and unique, put a price tag that reinforces that fact.

    When people pay money for something – regardless of whether it is an online course, a product or an experience – they tend to assign more value to their purchase. This encourages them to complete the course and truly imbibe the benefits it offers. This positive experience turns the students into happy customers and happy customers give rise to more mouth referrals. Hence, proper pricing can lead to a wider sales funnel.

  2. "My course is not as long as my competitors', I should charge less than the average price."

    3. Many online instructors think that they need to create a very long course in order to set a high price for the course.

    This is a common misconception.

    The length of your online course should in no way be a determining factor in setting your course price. Your course should be priced based on the value of its content and not its length. Every teacher has a teaching style that is unique to that individual. One instructor might take a few weeks to teach a particular topic while another equally competent teacher might take a couple of days. Similarly, it does not matter if your course has 20 modules spanning 1 hour each or 10 modules spanning half an hour each. It is the value of the course material, course outcomes and the expertise you have incorporated in the lessons that decide the worth of the course.

  3. "If I charge less than my competitors, that will give me an advantage over my competition and increase my returns."

    4. Several online instructors who are venturing into the world of online education for the first time are tempted to slash the price of their courses thinking this move will reward them with significant returns. However, the experience of many course creators has proved otherwise.

    If you price your course low then you’re harming its perceived value. Your customers will assume that your course is not worth a higher price. These customers form the section of people who are serious about acquiring new skills and are ready to pay a premium price in exchange for quality education that is delivered in an effective and organized manner under the guidance of the instructor. Therefore, it is not a good business decision to intentionally price your course lower than its actual value.

    Besides, if you really think about it, selling a low-priced course requires just as much effort as selling a higher-priced course. You would probably have the same marketing strategy too! The process of you guiding someone through to purchase a lower-priced course won’t vary a lot from the process of you guiding them through to buy a higher-priced one. You would be losing out on capitalising on your efforts and gaining a higher revenue if you underprice your course.

5. Provide different Pricing Structures

People love having multiple options and choosing the alternative which they think is most favourable to their needs. The same psychological process applies to the time when your prospective students are presented with multiple course options at different price points. Also, people have different goals while signing up for an online course. Some may want only the fundamental knowledge pertaining to that subject while others might be looking for a more comprehensive guide. It is important that you take this issue into consideration while forming your pricing strategy. To navigate this issue we suggest you offer multiple price points to your target market. For instance, you can provide the following options:

  1. Free Mini-Course:

    2. You can formulate this free mini-course by extracting a part of the valuable content from your course and packaging it as teaser content, a sample course that interested students can opt for. This sample content should contain only the basics of your course with little to no engaging features such as practical experiments or case studies.

    Mini-courses are also great for lead generation as well as for increasing your online audience. (Read: How to Grow Your Online Audience) (INSERT LINK HERE)

  2. Upgraded Version:

    3. The upgraded version should include all the features of the free mini-course along with direct access to downloadable resources and a certificate of completion.

  3. Premium Version:

    4. The Premium version of your course would include all features of the upgraded version in addition to live sessions for teacher-student and student-student interaction.

    The best part about offering different pricing tiers is that no matter which of your price options people choose, you still end up gaining a customer!

6. Keep Testing Your Price Point

The process of pricing is not set in stone, it is a dynamic part of any business. It is almost a given that as a course creator you would need to try out different price points in the initial stages.

While testing different price points, it is advised that you should start with a low price and then gradually attempt a price increase. Keep increasing the price till the time your sales volume starts plateauing. If all other variables were kept the same, this would imply that you have approached the peak value of what your target market is willing to spend for your course.

Gradually, with the help of all the tips mentioned above and your own learning experience, you will make correct estimations with respect to the pricing strategy of your online course.

Final Words

Now that you have a comprehensive idea about what the process of pricing an online course entails, go ahead and set a price tag for your own course! Follow these tips and we are sure that you would see results in no time at all! It is important that you trust your reasoning as well as this process when you set forth in this online course pricing journey. All the best!
