How to Grow Your Online Audience

By lorem ipsum
Nov 10,2021

Are you searching for ways to build and grow your online audience? Whether you are an educator who is in the planning phase of their online course creation journey or one who has just finished creating their course after investing several weeks and months into it, it is never too early or too late to look for strategies to grow your audience.

Why do you need to build an audience for your online course?

You might have created a truly incredible course, the best in the current market, however, if you cannot make it accessible to your potential students the purpose of providing quality education gets defeated. Regardless of the subject that your course is based on, it is important that you engage the segment of the audience who might be prospective customers. To increase the success rate of your course you should make significant efforts towards growing your online audience.

To help you out with your audience building strategies, we have compiled the following few tips that can greatly help you achieve your goals.

1. Research your Target Audience

This is one of the most crucial steps you need to follow if you are trying to operate a successful business. Make sure to ask yourself the following questions while you are researching the type of audience you are seeking:

  • Who is your ideal client?

Get information about the demographic, their income levels, what their goals are and how you can help them achieve those goals.

  • How can I target this audience?

Acquire information about your target audience's preferred mode of communication and what social media platforms such as Facebook, LinkedIn, Instagram etc. they are active on.

You can gather these insights by making use of Google Analytics( google analytics )service and/or social media channels, for example, you can discover important audience data by scouting the social media channels of your competitors and get information such as their follower locations, demographics and brand mentions. Another productive way of gaining insights would be to conduct surveys or polls to get direct feedback from your followers or email subscribers.

The goal here is to get a thorough character profile of your target profile so that you can reach them more efficiently in order to market your course. It is great if you already have access to a sizeable audience, but it is essential that you target the right people. These are the people who are likely to get converted from being potential customers to loyal customers.

2. Start a Blog to Benefit from Search Engine Optimization

Nowadays, what do most people do when they want an answer to their query?

You probably guessed it, they conduct a Google search.

Google is the world's most used search engine and naturally has massive web traffic. The inevitable downside to this fact is that every business wants this traffic, thus making organic search highly competitive. In order to stand out from the crowd, many course creators have realized the importance of Search Engine Optimization (SEO). Since you are offering an educational service, it is likely that the traffic you will be getting will be extremely targeted. That is to say, if people are searching for an online course on, say, JAVA programming they are interested in enrolling in the course that best suits their needs which makes them prospective customers. Hence, it is of utmost importance that you invest your efforts in understanding and implementing SEO.

One of the best ways of getting a higher ranking in search engine results is by starting a blog about your digital product. Blogging is good for SEO because it helps with a number of important ranking factors. If your blog content has high-quality material on topics that are relevant to your target audience, the overall performance of your website on search engines can significantly increase. If you want in-depth reasoning of why blogs are helpful for SEO take a look at Alex Chris's blog post titled "Blogging Helps SEO a Lot and Here is the Proof" [1].

You should write blogs on your own website, but also publish them on other people's websites. Guest blogging is also an excellent device in case you do not have your own website. Your blog content can help you grow your online course audience and might even attract new email subscribers or customers. Make sure that you mention the unique benefits of signing up for your online course in the blog, provide a link for the registration and also an option for signing up to your email list.

3. Build Your Email List & Step-up Your Email Marketing Campaign

Email marketing refers to the use of emails to convert leads into sales by sending personalized business messages to the target audience. It is a budget-friendly way of reaching out to your target audience and developing a productive client relationship with them. Email marketing boasts a 4200% ROI, that is, for every $1 you spend you get a staggering return of $42 [2]. Therefore, many course creators involve it in their marketing strategies.

An important prerequisite of any email campaign is to have an extensive email list. An email list, as the name suggests, is a list of email addresses of people who have opted to receive information from you via emails. In order to cultivate a good business relationship with your email subscribers, you need to make sure you leverage your email list in the right manner. It is imperative that you provide them with relevant and valuable content which does not seem overly promotional.

Your email list can also be used if you have updates on online product launches or discounts to share. You can also use it to collect useful feedback from the people who have already purchased or to conduct an opinion poll on how many times the email subscribers would like to receive promotional content. Another good content marketing strategy is to use emails to provide teaser content to your subscribers and encourage them to opt for the paid content if they are interested.

Email marketing is a remarkable communication medium but it is easy to get confused about how to best use it to sell your course. If you need some assistance you can opt for using email marketing tools. A basic email marketing tool offers services to design your email template, create an opt-in form, manage your email lists, track the analytics of your campaigns among other useful services. Some of the best-paid versions of email marketing tools offer all the basic features in addition to email automation and AI-powered analytics. We have a detailed post about the best marketing email marketing tools BEST MARKETING TOOLS in the market that throws light on what email marketing is and also on the pros and cons of the different email marketing tools.

If you are a complete novice when it comes to content marketing through emails it is unlikely you can learn the traits of the trade overnight. Devote time and, if your budget permits, money to understand and successfully implement an effective email marketing strategy. But in the meantime, here are some quick tips:

i) Always Personalize Your Emails:

-The most common way to personalize emails is to add the receiver’s name to each email, even while sending bulk emails.

-Another way could be to send different emails to different email subscribers depending on where they are in the "Subscribers Into Customers" journey.

ii) Create a Catchy Subject Line

While writing the subject line, keep the following pointers in mind:

-Keep it simple and brief

-Make sure it captures the gist of the purpose of the email

-Use questions to generate interest

-Personalize it to make the receiver feel more important

iii) Insert Graphics

-Images can lead to significantly better email performance. They allow you to strengthen your message and include information that can’t be communicated through text alone.

- Video content can greatly help pique the interest of the recipient and makes the purpose of the email easily understandable.

4. Create your Presence on Niche Social Networks

A niche social network is a platform that focuses on a specific interest group or demographic. They are different from the popular social media platforms of Facebook, Instagram, YouTube, and LinkedIn, as they do not cater to all audiences but to a particular subset of people. Because niche social networks are concentrated on a specific interest or group, they can provide relevant resources and audiences.

As course creators, you can choose the niche social media platforms according to your course topic. For instance, if you have created a course on graphic design then it is a good idea to establish your presence on platforms such as Behance , which was created for creative professionals to communicate, showcase and advertise their work.

Q&A sites such as Quora and Yahoo Answers and discussion forums such as Reddit are also excellent venues to interact with your niche audience and provide them with valuable content free of cost. You can provide alluring offers like a discount on the course to the first 10 people who sign up. Answering questions that are relevant to your niche can work in your favour as the users of these platforms might be interested in checking out your website or giving their email addresses to receive more details about your course.

5. Create Podcasts about the Topic of Your Course

A podcast is an online audio broadcast. Unlike a video or a blog post, a podcast does away with the visual element hence in that way, it is much easier to consume. Consumers list various reasons to listen to a podcast but the top reason is to gain knowledge. According to a report by Statista, around 75% of podcast consumers in the US say they enjoy tuning in to podcasts to learn new things and do so on a monthly basis [3]. Interestingly, according to a report by eMarketer, statistics show that 54% of podcast listeners are either somewhat or much more likely to consider buying from a brand after hearing its advertisement on a podcast [4].

Podcasts can serve as a valuable marketing tool for your online course. Starting a podcast can help build your credibility among your target audience and also other professionals in your industry. For example, let's assume you are a subject matter expert in political science. Through a podcast, you can share your expertise with interested consumers. You can even offer short lectures, interesting insights and practical tips to excel in a career in political science. The more people you help, the more credibility you gain. You can now use this opportunity to ask your listeners to sign up for your email list and to encourage them to take your online course.

6. Offer a Free Mini-course

One of the best ways to increase your audience size for your online course is by offering a "mini-course". This is essentially equivalent to a free trial period or the free teaser content that many online business platforms offer.

In simple terms, you would be taking some of the quality content from your main course, and offering it at no cost, in exchange for the email address of the user.

After you do offer a free mini-course to prospective students, things can go 2 ways:

  1. The first is that users will appreciate the free sample and want to sign up for a more comprehensive paid learning program with you thus turning subscribers into customers.
  2. The other thing that can happen is that users might decide that the online course product you are offering is not what they want or that the mini-course was sufficient enough for them. However, if a user gets results from your course they are sure to recommend your course to other people in their circle. Hence, you would be witnessing audience growth through mouth referral alone.

Conclusion:

Course content creation and building a sizeable audience are both important processes in the online course business. It is wise to take your time with creating quality content for your course while also focusing on your efforts to build an audience of potential customers. It won't hurt to have an audience ready even before your online course launches. Keep in mind all the points that we discussed in this article and work on analyzing your online marketing campaigns until they yield the desired results.
