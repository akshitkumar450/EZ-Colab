Benefits of Using Collaboration Software to Engage Your Students

By lorem ipsum
Nov 10,2021

In most virtual classrooms a single teacher is seen instructing several students. While teachers often work independently, they cannot — and should not — operate alone. Merely giving out the lessons for the day and assigning homework is not enough for the day to be deemed completed. In order to facilitate an effective online learning experience, teachers must take a student-centric approach in their teaching methods. The student-centric method of teaching (LINK TO EZCOLAB BLOG ON STUDENT-CENTRIC ONLINE LEARNING) refers to instructional approaches or academic support strategies that are created to address the learning needs and interests of individual students as well as the entire community of students. This approach majorly focuses on collaborative activities and has been widely praised and adopted by numerous members of the teaching domain.

Collaborative learning enables teachers and students to share, communicate, and work together on common tasks by sharing ideas, information and documents with each other. The foundational value behind collaborative activities is that when people come together to achieve a common goal they learn better and perform better. Interactions such as a teacher collaborating with other teachers, a student collaborating with other students or even a student-teacher collaboration for a specific task go a long way in ensuring that everyone partakes and also that the final goal is met.

What is Collaborative Software?

To facilitate collaborative activities certain software tools called groupware or collaborative software are available. This software ensures that communication and coordination can occur in a streamlined process so as to ensure a highly efficient virtual educational environment. If you are a teacher and are contemplating whether you should avail the services of collaborative software to increase the engagement value for your students, you are in the right place!

We have compiled a list of the incredible benefits that using a collaborative software can provide— not only for teachers such as yourself but also for your students:

1. Reduction in Feeling of Isolation between Teachers and Students:

One of the most frequently noted criticisms that digital learning receives is the lack of communication between the students and the teacher. The current methods being employed in virtual learning do not facilitate teacher-pupil interaction which can result in the feeling of social isolation. There is often no provision of a digital space where the online students can virtually assemble to have a productive interaction with their instructor or each other. Various research reports have shown that when students are presented with limited opportunities for communication with their teachers and peers it results in weakened morale, which can further lead to lower performance. One such report was authored by Peter Shea named "Bridges and barriers to teaching online college courses: A study of experienced online faculty in thirty-six colleges" [1].

To combat this obstacle in the students' learning process many teachers prefer to opt for the help of collaborative software. Collaborative learning has been shown to develop higher-order thinking skills in students and boost their self-esteem as well. Engaging the students in group projects and asking for their innovative ideas and opinions on the lessons can maximize the educational experience by demonstrating the material while improving social and interpersonal skills. Student collaboration also leads to community building which greatly motivates them to continue learning with enthusiasm.

2. Ease of Content Sharing

A good collaboration software enables a teacher to create numerous files such as lesson plans, knowledge bases, home assignments, projects and facilitates an easy sharing of such diverse forms of media with the students who have access to the software. This negates the need to use emails or other messaging applications such as WhatsApp, Telegram etc every time some course material is to be distributed. One of the biggest advantages of having a collaborative platform for content sharing is that it ensures that every student will have access to the latest version of the course material and that there are no unnecessary repetitions.

3. Aids in Professional Development of Instructors

Digital learning practices continuously evolve, which means that online course creators need to keep adapting and improving as well. In a research on the benefits of collaboration for educators, which was led by Deborah Butler, it was concluded that successful collaboration led to teachers reflecting on their method of instruction, making positive changes to their teaching practice and gaining new knowledge about the importance of student engagement [2]. If teachers partake in collaborative activities it can also help them in utilizing formative assessments to make sustained changes to their method of instruction depending on the student response. Virtual collaboration with fellow teachers or students can help the teacher in gaining new perspectives that can make their online course worth signing up for.

As a collaborative tool, EZColab is committed to facilitating hassle-free communication between the teacher and the student community in an online environment. This enormously benefits both parties as the teacher gets to interact with the online learners and the students start feeling more comfortable with their teachers and also starts building a productive relationship with them. The Ideas Portal feature at EZColab is another student collaboration tool that provides the students with a space to voice their creative ideas or suggest improvements to the course material. Fellow students can then show their support for this idea and teachers will immediately be notified. This will aid the teachers to gain exposure to diverse perspectives and challenge them to incorporate changes to better suit the majority of their students.

4. Aids in Personal Development of Students

A traditional classroom effectively promotes teacher-student interaction and student-student interaction. In order to stimulate a positive learning environment in virtual classrooms, student engagement is of utmost importance. The inclusion of collaborative tools in an online course leads to positive student performance. Collaborative group interactions promote active learning, shared knowledge base, and peer-to-peer interaction. This consequently helps build a foundation for learning. Collaboration also teaches the students how to work well with others in real-life situations. Collaborative projects help train online learners for the modern workplace where teamwork is key. This also helps in the development of soft skills of the students such as:

  1. Learning how to voice opinions and express ideas
  2. Time management
  3. Development of higher-order thinking skills
  4. Increased retentivity
  5. Higher self-esteem
